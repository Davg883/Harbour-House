A Brand Rooted in Place

Harbour House is not just a venue; it is an extension of the Ryde Marina waterfront itself. The brand's identity is anchored in its physical location—perched directly on the harbour wall, with views spanning boats, masts, open Solent skies, and the daily sunset.

This section serves to align staff and external agencies on our three core brand assets:

  • Front Elevation: The iconic clapboard facade and triangular gables representing the building's structural footprint.
  • Harbour-Facing Location: Framing the venue against boats, marina walls, and water.
  • Guest Viewpoint: Promoting photography taken from bar-side tables looking out, placing the guest directly in the experience.

Typography System

Resilient stack fallback system. Fonts load from Google CDN, but immediately degrade gracefully to pre-approved system fonts if offline.

Display font (Headlines, titles) - Playfair Display
Playfair Display: Beautiful Editorial Headlines

font-family: 'Playfair Display', Georgia, 'Times New Roman', serif;

Body Text (UI elements, descriptions) - Montserrat
Montserrat: Clean, modern, highly legible sans-serif for UI menus, pricing sheets, and text paragraphs.

font-family: 'Montserrat', system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif;

Script font (Short accents only) - Caveat
Caveat: Handcrafted coastal accents

font-family: 'Caveat', 'Segoe Print', 'Bradley Hand', 'Brush Script MT', cursive;

Rule: script font should only be used for short decorative word accents (e.g. "by the sea", "established 2024"). Important booking details or menus must remain in serif or sans-serif.

Occasion Copy Guidelines

Ensure all marketing text, emails, and group responses align with the brand guidelines. Reference the comparison below:

✔ Preferred (Premium & Relaxed)

"Gather around the table on the harbour wall. We accommodate birthdays, team catch-ups, and socials with custom boards and sunset drinks packages."

✘ Avoid (Cheap & Loud)

"BOOK YOUR WEDDING OR BIG PARTY HERE! We fit 100+ people. Cheap deals available! First come first served."

Why: The preferred copy feels inclusive and descriptive, highlighting the setting. Avoid copy is aggressive, refers to weddings (which the venue does not host), and cheapens the brand identity.
